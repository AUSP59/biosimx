# BioSimX — Code of Conduct (v1.0)

## Our Pledge
We, the maintainers and contributors of BioSimX, pledge to create a safe, welcoming, and professional community for everyone. We are committed to a harassment-free experience regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion (or lack thereof), sexual identity and orientation, socioeconomic status, or technical choices.

## Our Standards

### Positive behaviors (expected)
- Demonstrate empathy, respect, and kindness toward other people.
- Assume good intent; ask for clarification before jumping to conclusions.
- Be constructive in reviews; focus on the work, not the person.
- Use inclusive, accessible language; prefer plain English and clear reasoning.
- Respect different viewpoints and experiences; seek consensus and compromise.
- Acknowledge and gracefully accept constructive feedback.
- Prioritize safety, privacy, and the well-being of the community.

### Unacceptable behaviors (never allowed)
- Harassment, discrimination, or hateful conduct in any form.
- Public or private harassment, including stalking or sustained disruption.
- Sexualized language or imagery; sexual attention or advances of any kind.
- Insults, trolling, derogatory comments, personal or political attacks.
- Doxing: publishing others’ private information without explicit permission.
- Threats of violence, intimidation, or encouragement of self-harm.
- Unwanted contact after a request to stop.
- Unauthorized recordings of private meetings or non-public materials.
- Abuse of reporting tools (e.g., false or bad-faith reports).
- Any other conduct that a reasonable person would consider inappropriate.

## Scope
This Code applies to all community spaces and activities, including but not limited to: the GitHub organization and repository (issues, pull requests, discussions, wikis), code reviews, commit messages, releases, documentation, community calls, conferences, workshops, and any public or private communications related to the project. It also applies when an individual is representing the project (e.g., using an official account or affiliation).

## Responsibilities of Maintainers
Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful. Maintainers must:
- Act fairly, consistently, and promptly.
- Keep reports as confidential as possible.
- Recuse themselves from decisions where they have a conflict of interest.
- Provide clear explanations of actions when appropriate and possible.

## Reporting an Incident

### Where to report
- Primary contact (Lead Maintainer): AUSP59  
- Email: alanursapu@gmail.com  
- Alternatively, open a private report via GitHub “Report abuse” tools or contact a maintainer via direct message if available.

If you or another person are in immediate danger, contact local emergency services first.

### What to include
- Your contact information (optional but helpful for follow-up).
- Names of the individuals involved, including witnesses where possible.
- When and where the incident occurred (dates, times, URLs, channels).
- A detailed description of what happened; include screenshots or logs when relevant.
- Any prior context and any steps already taken.

### Our commitment to confidentiality
We will respect the reporter’s privacy and will not publish identifying information without explicit consent, except when required by law or when there is an immediate risk to safety.

### Response timeline
- Acknowledge receipt within 72 hours.
- Provide an initial assessment and next steps within 7 days.
- Share resolution outcome when completed (timelines may vary with complexity).

## Enforcement Guidelines
Maintainers will follow these Community Impact Guidelines when determining consequences:

1) Correction  
- **Impact:** Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional or unwelcome, one-off.  
- **Consequence:** A private, written warning and guidance on expected behavior.

2) Warning  
- **Impact:** A violation after receiving a correction; or moderately severe misconduct.  
- **Consequence:** A formal warning and requirement to modify the behavior. May include temporary restrictions (e.g., muted in discussions).

3) Temporary Suspension  
- **Impact:** Serious violation or repeated problematic behavior.  
- **Consequence:** Temporary ban from interaction with project spaces and activities for a defined period, with conditions for reinstatement.

4) Permanent Ban  
- **Impact:** Persistent refusal to comply, severe harassment, threats of violence, doxing, or other egregious violations.  
- **Consequence:** Permanent removal from all project spaces and activities.

Enforcement actions may be combined with other remedies (e.g., removing content, reverting commits, limiting repository permissions).

## Appeals
If you believe an enforcement action was made in error or disproportionate to the violation, you may appeal by contacting the maintainers (alanursapu@gmail.com) within 14 days of the decision. Appeals should include:
- The decision being appealed and date.
- A concise statement of reasons for appeal (procedural error, new facts, disproportionate action).
- Any supporting evidence.

Appeals are reviewed by maintainers not involved in the original decision (when possible). Decisions on appeal are final for that case.

## Conflicts of Interest
Maintainers must disclose any potential conflicts (personal, professional, financial) that could influence their impartiality. They must recuse themselves from handling related reports or votes.

## Retaliation & False Reporting
- Retaliation against someone who reports a concern or participates in an investigation is strictly prohibited.  
- Knowingly false or malicious reports are themselves a violation and will be handled accordingly.

## Moderation Tools & Record-Keeping
- Reports and enforcement decisions will be recorded in a private log accessible only to maintainers with a need to know.  
- Data will be retained only as long as necessary for enforcement and legal compliance.

## Accessibility & Inclusivity
We strive to make community spaces accessible. If you need accommodations (e.g., communication preferences, formats, captioning), contact the maintainers; we will make reasonable efforts to support your participation.

## Updates & Changelog
This Code may be updated to reflect community needs, legal requirements, or best practices. Substantive changes will be announced in the repository (e.g., via CHANGELOG, Releases, or Discussions), with a summary of modifications.
